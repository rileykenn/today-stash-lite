What Makes a Local Deal Actually Worth Using?
Not all deals are created equal. Before we get into where to find them, it's worth understanding what separates a genuine local deal from a gimmick.
It's available in-store, not just online
A real local deal is one you redeem at the business — not on their website or a third-party app. In-store redemption means the saving is tied to actually visiting the venue, which is good for you (you get the experience) and good for them (they get foot traffic).
It's offered by the business directly, not a middleman
The best local deals come from businesses themselves — not from a national aggregator who's taken a clip. When a local café or gym creates their own offer on a platform like Today's Stash, they control the terms, the timing and how often it can be used. That means the offer is deliberate and meaningful, not a lazy 5% off to bulk up a database.
It's time-sensitive or limited in quantity
Deals that are available 365 days a year aren't really deals — they're just the price. Good local promotions are tied to quieter trading periods, special occasions or a limited number of redemptions per day. This is what makes them genuinely valuable rather than background noise.
It doesn't require you to print anything, show a screenshot or explain yourself to staff
This might sound minor, but it matters enormously at the counter. The best redemption experience is invisible to the staff and effortless for the customer. Today's Stash uses a QR code on the business's counter — you scan it from your phone's browser, the offer is confirmed, and that's it. No screenshots, no login screens, no awkward conversations.
